Federated Hub & Spoke:
Model Structure
Slide 02

The Federated Hub & Spoke model places a central governance unit (the Hub) at the core of the organisation. Domain-specific units (the Spokes) retain operational autonomy while adhering to standards, platforms, and policies set by the Hub. This structure eliminates duplication, accelerates delivery, and ensures enterprise-wide coherence.

Central Hub — Digital Transformation

Sets AI & data governance frameworks, platforms, and shared services. Acts as the enabling engine for all domain units.

Domain Spokes — Executing Units

Each business unit retains local analytics & BI capability. Spokes consume platforms & standards from the Hub, not duplicate them.

Unified Governance Layer

A shared steering committee oversees the entire portfolio. Joint KPIs, data policies, and AI ethics apply across all spokes equally.

Bidirectional Flow of Value

The Hub provides platforms, AI tools, and standards downward. Spokes provide domain data, use cases, and insights upward to the Hub.

Scalable AI Enablement

The AI function sits within the Hub as an enabler — not as an independent silo. It serves all domain spokes through shared APIs, model libraries, and tooling.

Federated Accountability

Domain units own their outcomes while sharing the infrastructure. This prevents bottlenecks and empowers local decision-making within defined guardrails.

Efficiency Without Duplication

Shared AI platforms, data catalogs, and governance frameworks reduce cost and fragmentation. Every spoke benefits from enterprise-level capability without building it independently.

Core principle: The Hub governs, enables, and provides. The Spokes execute, deliver, and feed insights back. Together they form a self-reinforcing system that grows stronger as adoption scales.
