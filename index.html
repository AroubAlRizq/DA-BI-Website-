Slide 02
🏛️

Federated Hub & Spoke

Central BI/AI governance with domain-embedded analytics teams. Adopted by Gartner-rated government entities (UAE TDRA, Saudi NDA, UK GDS). Prevents duplication; preserves domain autonomy.

🧩

Centre of Excellence (CoE)

Dedicated AI CoE sets standards, platforms, and frameworks. BI Centers focus on analytics delivery. Model used by McKinsey & Deloitte client frameworks and OECD digital government guidance.

⚙️

Platform-Led Model

AI Unit owns the technology layer (LLMOps, MLOps, platforms). BI Center consumes services for analytics products. Emerging trend in large-scale digital transformation programs (Singapore, KSA).

📊

Data-First Backbone

BI Center owns the data layer (governance, quality, master data). AI Unit builds on this foundation. Recommended by MIT CDOIQ and World Economic Forum data governance frameworks.

🤝

Joint Delivery Model

Shared delivery squads for AI-powered analytics products. Combines BI data expertise with AI engineering. Reduces silos through embedded cross-functional product teams.

🔑

Key Principle

Separation of concerns: BI Center = data & analytics delivery. AI Unit = enablement, governance, and platforms. Collaboration at defined interfaces — not duplication.

Global consensus: Best-practice organizations assign data + analytics ownership to BI Centers and AI governance + enablement to AI Units, with clear interfaces at model development, platform usage, and policy adherence.
