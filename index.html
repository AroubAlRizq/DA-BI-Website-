Slide 02
🏛️

Centralized Model

A single unit oversees all data, BI, and AI initiatives. Ensures consistency and integration of efforts across the organization. Common in early-stage digital transformation programs.

🔀

Decentralized Model

Individual departments manage their own data and analytics efforts. Promotes agility and local responsiveness but may lead to siloed data, duplicated tools, and inconsistent governance.

★ Best Practice
⚙️

Hybrid — Hub & Spoke

Centralized AI governance and platforms (Hub — AI Unit) combined with decentralized analytics delivery (Spoke — BI Centers). Balances oversight with domain autonomy. Adopted by UAE TDRA, UK GDS, OECD frameworks.

🧩

Centre of Excellence (CoE)

Dedicated AI CoE sets standards, platforms, and frameworks. BI Centers focus on analytics delivery. Used by McKinsey & Deloitte client frameworks and OECD digital government guidance.

📊

Data-First Backbone

BI Center owns the data layer — governance, quality, master data. AI Unit builds on this foundation. Recommended by MIT CDOIQ and World Economic Forum data governance frameworks.

🔑

Key Principle

Separation of concerns: BI Center = data & analytics delivery. AI Unit = enablement, governance & platforms. Collaboration at defined interfaces — not duplication.

Global consensus: Best-practice organizations assign data + analytics ownership to BI Centers and AI governance + enablement to AI Units, with clear interfaces at model development, platform usage, and policy adherence.
